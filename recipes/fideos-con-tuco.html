Dr. Chapatin's "Fideos con Tuco"

A classic Argentinian recipe for pasta lovers.

"Tuco" is the name of a traditional sauce from countries such as Uruguay, Argentina and Chile, prepared from a tomato base. It's commonly used as a companion for spaghetti, but also for other pasta dishes such as gnocci.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg of minced meat
  • 2 onions
  • 100 ml of sunflower oil
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 leaves of bay tree laurel
  • 1 spoonful of oregano
  • 2 spoonfuls of tomato extract
  • 200 grams of tomato puree
  • a pinch of salt
  • a pinch of pepper
  • ground pepper
  • a pack of spaghetti
  • parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Pour a thin layer of oil into a pot. Add the meat to the pot and start heating, chop the onions and garlic into small pieces. Add them to the pot, stirring well with a wooden spoon. Once the onions have turned golden brown, you must add the laurel and oregano leaves.
  2. Add the tomato puree and extract. Add the salt and pepper and keep stirring. Allow the preparation to reach a boil and turn the fire to minimum.
  3. If needed you could add some water at this point to keep the sauce from sticking to the pot and burning. Don't overdo it however: Tuco should always remain a thick sauce.
  4. Whilst the Tuco is still cooking, add water to a large pot and let it boil. Add a pinch of salt to this water and cook the pasta in it. Once it's done, strain the spaghetti and serve it on a deep plate, adding the Tuco (still boiling hot) on top.
  5. Grind the parmessan cheese and prinkle it on your plate at leasure.
